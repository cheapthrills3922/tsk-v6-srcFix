Chamoli

Description

Chamoli is a town and district in the state of Uttarakhand. This city is part of the northern mountainous area. Its average height is approximately 4,500 feet, but some areas are higher than 10,000 feet. Chamoli is situated in the middle of the Himalayas. The Alaknanda River, which originates from the Zaskar range of Tibet, is famous here. The area of Chamoli is approximately 3,525 square miles. In the summer, the weather is cold and pleasant. The high peaks become snowy. Chamoli is surrounded by forests, which have an abundance of paddy, bangoke, oak, etc. Fruits are also plentiful here. In animal husbandry, sheep, goats, horses, and yaks are kept.

Best Time to Visit Chamoli

November to March and July to August are the best months to visit Chamoli. However, this place experiences fantastic weather throughout the year. If your purpose for visiting Chamoli is to see the Valley of Flowers, the best time to visit would be July to August. The summers are pleasant, with temperatures ranging between 15 to 30 degrees Celsius. The area receives snowfall in the winter, with temperatures dropping to freezing point. However, the monsoon season experiences excessive rain, causing the area to be prone to landslides.

How to Reach Chamoli

  • By Air - The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport, located 221 kilometers from Chamoli.
  • By Rail - The nearest railway station is Rishikesh.
  • By Road - Buses are available from Chamoli to Dehradun, Haridwar, Rishikesh, Nainital, and Almora.
