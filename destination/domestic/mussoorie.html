Mussoorie

Description

Mussoorie, positioned at a distance of 35 km from Dehradun, is an outstanding hill station in Uttarakhand, situated at a height of 2,005 meters above sea level. It is located at the foothills of the Himalayas and is known as the Queen of the Hills. Mussoorie is one of the top hill stations in India, well-known for weekend getaways, honeymoon destinations, Himalayan views, and Mall Road. The surrounding hills and greenery can be glimpsed from anywhere in Mussoorie. A Mussoorie tour is like golden amber, keeping the glimmer of rejuvenation kindled in the mortal body and immortal soul. These are some of the features that are instantly identifiable with Mussoorie, yet not the only aspects. Among the crown jewels of sightseeing in Uttarakhand, Mussoorie has its charm. The allure of this 'Queen of Hills' attracts everyone coming to this town nestled in the foothills of the Garhwal Himalayas; the earlier one visits, the more tolerant they become. While there is a huge need to control over-tourism in Mussoorie and promote its ecological aspects, the place continues to draw tourists from India and abroad, being easily accessible and enchantingly welcoming.

Best Time to Visit Mussoorie

The best time to visit Mussoorie is from September to June, making it suitable for all ages and groups of individuals.

How to Reach Mussoorie

  • By Air - Jolly Grant Airport is the nearest airport to Mussoorie. From Dehradun, which is adjacent to Mussoorie, you can hire a taxi to reach the hill station.
  • By Rail - Mussoorie is well linked with all the main cities by train, with the nearest railway station being Dehradun, as well as others like Haridwar, Ambala Cantt, Moradabad, and Delhi.
  • By Road - Buses from various locations like Delhi, Haridwar, Dehradun, Chandigarh, etc., are easily available. Hiring a car or taxi or taking your own car to Mussoorie is also a good plan if you are prepared for a road journey.
